⚖️ Solving One-Step Equations

An equation is a mathematical statement that two expressions are equal. To solve an equation means to find the value of the variable that makes it true.

Key Concept

Whatever you do to one side of an equation, you must do to the other. This keeps the equation balanced.

The Four Operations

You can solve one-step equations using inverse (opposite) operations:

  • If a number is added, subtract it from both sides
  • If a number is subtracted, add it to both sides
  • If a number is multiplied, divide both sides by it
  • If a number is divided, multiply both sides by it
✏️ Worked Example 1 — Addition
Solve:   x + 7 = 12
7 is being added to x. To undo addition, use subtraction.
  1. Identify the operation: 7 is being added to x
  2. Subtract 7 from both sides:   x + 7 − 7 = 12 − 7
  3. Simplify:   x = 5
  4. Check:   5 + 7 = 12 ✅
x + 7 = 12 x + 7 − 7 = 12 − 7 x = 5
✏️ Worked Example 2 — Multiplication
Solve:   4x = 28
4 is being multiplied by x. To undo multiplication, divide both sides by 4.
  1. Identify the operation: x is being multiplied by 4
  2. Divide both sides by 4:   4x ÷ 4 = 28 ÷ 4
  3. Simplify:   x = 7
  4. Check:   4 × 7 = 28 ✅
4x = 28 4x ÷ 4 = 28 ÷ 4 x = 7

🔢 Multi-Step Equations

When an equation has multiple operations, you solve it by working in reverse order of operations — undo addition/subtraction first, then multiplication/division.

Key Concept

Always undo addition and subtraction before multiplication and division. Think of it as "peeling the onion" from the outside in.

Two-Step Equations

The form ax + b = c is the most common. Follow these two steps every time:

  1. Add or subtract the constant (b) from both sides
  2. Multiply or divide both sides by the coefficient (a)
✏️ Worked Example 1 — Two-Step
Solve:   2x + 4 = 10
First remove the +4 by subtracting 4 from both sides. Then deal with the 2x.
  1. Subtract 4 from both sides:   2x + 4 − 4 = 10 − 4 → 2x = 6
  2. Divide both sides by 2:   2x ÷ 2 = 6 ÷ 2 → x = 3
  3. Check:   2(3) + 4 = 6 + 4 = 10 ✅
2x + 4 = 10 2x + 4 − 4 = 10 − 4 2x = 6 2x ÷ 2 = 6 ÷ 2 x = 3
✏️ Worked Example 2 — Variables on Both Sides
Solve:   5x − 3 = 2x + 9
Collect all x terms on one side first by subtracting 2x from both sides.
  1. Subtract 2x from both sides:   3x − 3 = 9
  2. Add 3 to both sides:   3x = 12
  3. Divide both sides by 3:   x = 4
  4. Check:   5(4) − 3 = 17  and  2(4) + 9 = 17 ✅
5x − 3 = 2x + 9 5x − 2x − 3 = 2x − 2x + 9 3x − 3 = 9 3x = 12 x = 4

📊 Solving Inequalities

Inequalities are like equations, but instead of = they use <, >, ≤, or ≥. You solve them almost the same way — with one important exception.

⚠️ Critical Rule

When you multiply or divide both sides of an inequality by a negative number, you must flip the inequality sign.

Inequality Symbols

  • < — less than (open circle on number line)
  • > — greater than (open circle on number line)
  • ≤ — less than or equal to (closed circle on number line)
  • ≥ — greater than or equal to (closed circle on number line)
✏️ Worked Example 1 — Basic Inequality
Solve and graph:   x + 3 > 7
Treat it like an equation — subtract 3 from both sides. The sign stays the same.
  1. Subtract 3 from both sides:   x + 3 − 3 > 7 − 3
  2. Simplify:   x > 4
  3. Graph: open circle at 4, arrow pointing right →
x + 3 > 7 x > 4 Graph: ○────────> (open circle at 4)
✏️ Worked Example 2 — Flip the Sign!
Solve:   −3x ≤ 12
You need to divide by −3. Since it's negative, flip the ≤ to ≥!
  1. Divide both sides by −3 (NEGATIVE → flip the sign!)
  2. −3x ÷ (−3) ≥ 12 ÷ (−3)
  3. x ≥ −4
  4. Graph: closed circle at −4, arrow pointing right →
−3x ≤ 12 ÷(−3) on both sides → FLIP SIGN x ≥ −4 Graph: ●────────> (closed circle at −4)
